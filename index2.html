Candace Parker

"THE GOAT"

Candace Parker is a prominent American professional basketball player known for her versatility, skill, and impact on the game. Born on April 19, 1986, in St. Louis, Missouri, she quickly rose to prominence as a high school basketball star, earning national attention for her outstanding performance. Parker played college basketball at the University of Tennessee under the legendary coach Pat Summitt, where she led the Lady Volunteers to two NCAA championships in 2007 and 2008. Her exceptional play earned her numerous accolades, including the prestigious Naismith College Player of the Year award twice.

In 2008, Parker was the first overall pick in the WNBA Draft by the Los Angeles Sparks. She made an immediate impact in the league, winning both the Rookie of the Year and the MVP awards in her debut season. Throughout her career, Parker has been celebrated for her all-around game, excelling in scoring, rebounding, and defense. She led the Sparks to a WNBA championship in 2016 and has been selected to multiple All-Star teams. Beyond the WNBA, Parker has also enjoyed success on the international stage, winning gold medals with Team USA at the 2008 and 2012 Olympics. Off the court, she is known for her work as a broadcaster and advocate for various social issues.
